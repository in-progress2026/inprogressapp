001 March 2026 Manifesto

The Gap Between
The DAW And The Stream

While creating you listen to your music in your DAW.

When I used to work in music, I loved to test the music in different speakers and in different headphones. I used to overanalyze every EQ change and how it sounded everywhere.

It was so annoying to keep doing that in the Files App. It was even worse to use WhatsApp. I had to remember where every file was, who sent me, when I saved it. So confusing. It lacks dignity.

There was a mess of files of unfinished music, that I was too lazy to organize, because it was hard and not intuitive to organize.

There is a huge gap between the DAW and the Streaming Service. That gap is slowly being fixed. There are amazing tools out there.

However, most of them are just way too complicated. They ask you for subscriptions; they have useless features; and they don't even look that good.

I created In Progress not because there were no alternatives — I created it because there were no good alternatives.

Why would I rent a tool to listen to what I own?

Why would I pay per month for something an early 2000's mp3 player did for 20 bucks?

This is 2026. I wanted a good audio player for local files, and I think I built a good audio player for local files.

  • Organizing files by nesting Folders and Playlists is an OCD paradise.
  • Analyzing the Key to the songs is a must-have feature for musicians who don't know how to get there by ear.
  • Adding a manual Queue is an amazing way to have control over your playback experience.
  • Allowing users to share entire playlists via AirDrop, completely offline — keeping not only the files, but also the custom names and order — is a feature I'm really proud of.

Download In Progress 1.2. Listen to your files with dignity. You won't be disappointed.

002 March 2026 Update

V1.2 —
The Sharing Update

Most music apps are built for streaming. But if you make music, you're constantly listening to your own demos, drafts, and unfinished ideas — and those tools just don't feel right.

V1.2 adds one thing I always wanted: you can now send full playlists to another musician via AirDrop — completely offline. It also works via WhatsApp or iMessage.

No cloud. No accounts. No syncing. Just files, like it should be.

I'm trying to make this feel less like a "music player" and more like a tool for working on your own music.

Also — someone emailed me saying the text was too small, so I added a Large Text setting just for that. I'm reading everything and adjusting as I go.

There is no developers team, no design team, no marketing team — just me, doing it in my free time.

It's €1.99, one-time. No subscriptions. Keep creating, and listen to your music with dignity.
